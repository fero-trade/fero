Background

Layo-Teraga Coffee Producers' Cooperative, shortly called Fero General Trading PLC, was established in 2005 by 60 smallholder coffee farmers (59 males and one female). Today, the Cooperative has grown to 1,814 members (1,383 males and 431 females).

The Cooperative operated under the Oromia Coffee Farmers’ Cooperative Union (OCFCU) until 2020. In 2021, it separated from OCFCU and began exporting its products independently.

Objective

Enhance global market presence by sustainably supplying high-quality highland coffee for the betterment of the socio-economic conditions of the members of the Cooperative.

Specific Objectives

  • Empowerment of members: Improving access to resources, knowledge, skills, participation and leadership, with special focus on women, youth and disadvantaged groups.
  • Market expansion: Identifying existing and new global markets and establishing strategic partnerships.
  • Quality control: Implementing up-to-date technology and scientific knowledge to meet global standards.
  • Capacity building: Providing regular training, education, infrastructure development and partnerships.
  • Community projects: Implementing need-based projects using Cooperative capital and stakeholder support.

Who We Are

Layo Teraga Cooperative is a multi-purpose cooperative located in Uraga District of Guji Zone, Oromia Regional Government, Ethiopia.

The Cooperative grows highland coffee in a traditional way, with deep respect for nature. We honor our ancestors who passed down sustainable farming systems that produce unique coffee flavors.

Fero General Trading PLC is committed to protecting the environment and delivering a better future for the world through hard work and integrity.

What We Do

  • Ensure sustainability: Preserving the sustainable production systems inherited from our ancestors.
  • Support fair trade: Ensuring equity among Cooperative members.
  • Quality control: Maintaining and improving coffee quality systems.
  • Innovation adoption: Adopting scientific knowledge and modern technologies.
  • Ensure engagement: Promoting inclusive participation of members and stakeholders.

Why Choose Us

  • Sustainability Certified: Supporting smallholder farmers and environmentally responsible production.
  • Organic Certified: Producing coffee without agro-chemical inputs, ensuring eco-friendly farming.
  • Community Impact: Reinvesting proceeds into education, healthcare and infrastructure development.

Our Duties and Responsibilities

  • Resource allocation: Providing tools, finance and technologies to members.
  • Training and education: Implementing need-based training programs.
  • Facilitation: Representing members in policy discussions and buyer negotiations.
  • Environmental protection: Ensuring coffee production follows nature-friendly practices.

Mission

Empower farmers with resources, training and fair pricing to produce high-quality highland coffee and access fair global markets.

Vision

Sustainably supply world-class highland coffee from a highly protected environment by empowering smallholder Cooperative members.

Our Values
